Explanation of Era Divisions

Historians of Mythage divide the timeline of the world into two distinct epochs: Before Fracture (BF) and After Fracture (AF).
The transition between these two eras was but a violent, world altering cataclysm that rewrote the laws of nature and the balance of power overnight.

The Fracture was a world shattering cataclysm that occurred over two millennia ago, violently tearing open the very fabric of reality. Massive, jagged rifts tore through the sky and earth, acting as cosmic floodgates that poured raw,chaotic magic known as Aether into a previously mundane world. Though these dimensional wounds have long since "scabbed over" and stabilized into glowing, permanent leylines, they forever altered the physical landscape, mutating ordinary wildlife into the terrifying magical beasts and monsters that inhabit the world in 2309 AF.

More crucially, the Fracture fundamentally rewrote the laws of power by abruptly weakening the mortal realm's connection to the Gods. Before the sky broke, supernatural abilities were entirely monopolized by deities who granted Blessings to the devout and Curses to the wicked. When the rifts opened and the heavens went completely silent, power was suddenly democratized. Magic became a tangible, environmental resource that anyone with the right knowledge could harvest and wield, instantly collapsing the old theocracies and laying the foundation for the magic fueled, feudal empires, societies and academies that rule the present day.The Gods are all but extinct, but no longer retain the monopoly they once used to. Following the rapture, new arts and even new gods have emerged, either birthed or out of hiding, revealing their colors to the new world.

Before Fracture (BF): The Age of Reverence

The BF timeline is marked by agonizingly slow societal progress. History was measured by the grand holy wars, the rise of saints, and the breeding of monsters from cursed bloodlines.

~1000 BF: The Founding of Covenants and the word of Old Gods

The earliest recorded history. The Pantheon of Gods directly communicates with the first mortal kings, establishing the rigid laws of morality and obedience. The first Blessings are bestowed upon devout champions, and the first Curses are branded upon sinners.

~650 BF: The Rise of the Grand Chantry

Various religious sects unify into the Grand Chantry, a monolithic theocracy worshipping the gods as a single pantheon. Kings are stripped of absolute authority, forced to rule only with the blessing of the High Priests. Theocracy establishes a monopoly on divine interpretation.

~400 BF: The Purge

Generations of cursed bloodlines have warped into monstrous forms (goblins, trolls, gorgons). The Chantry launches massive holy crusades, sending Blessed Paladins into the deep forests and mountains to exterminate these "Forsaken." It is a centuries-long war of attrition.

~50 BF: The Stagnation

The height of the Chantry's power. Massive cathedrals dominate city skylines. Technological innovation is practically non-existent; scholars who attempt to study the natural world rather than holy texts are branded heretics and stripped of their rights.

1 BF: The Beggining of the End

The Grand Chantry achieves absolute authority and power. In secret, the Chantry attempts to harness, or so to say "steal" divine energy through personal means. This is done through a series of unknown experiments and works, whose existence are known only by mentions in ancient manuscripts rarely found by archeologists. Yet, in an attempt to commit such heresy, the Chantry commits a fatal mistake. Omens appear across the world: holy fires in the temples burn blue, Blessed knights temporarily lose their invulnerability, and prayers go seemingly unheard. The High Priests assure the populace it is merely a test of faith.

Year Zero: The Cataclysm

0 AF: The Day of Fracture

The sky violently tears open in a cascade of blinding light and deafening sound. Jagged, weeping rifts appear across the continent. Raw, chaotic magic (Aether) pours into the mundane world. The voices of the Gods vanish entirely. The temples fall completely silent. Blessed individuals retain their powers, but no new Blessings are granted.

After Fracture (AF): The End of the Beggining

1 - 10 AF: The Decade of Ash

Total global panic. The Aether bleeding from the rifts causes massive environmental upheaval. The first instances of Rift-blight occur—animals and plants near the rifts undergo horrific, rapid mutations. The Chantry desperately tries to maintain order by executing anyone who approaches the rifts, calling the Aether "Demonic Rot."

14 AF: The First Spark

A penniless, unblessed scholar named Elara Vance discovers how to manipulate Aether using physical gestures, willpower, and geometric runes. She successfully conjures a flame without a prayer. Arcanism is born.

30 - 55 AF: The Aether Rebellions

Knowledge of Arcanism spreads like wildfire among the peasantry and the oppressed. For the first time, mortals can fight back against the Blessed elite. Violent uprisings tear down several theocratic kingdoms. The Chantry is fractured, retreating to highly fortified holy cities.

80 AF: The Founding of the Arcane Guilds

Magic becomes formalized. The first academies are built to study the Aether safely. Mages realize that the rifts are not just anomalies, but sources of incredibly valuable magical reagents and crystallized Aether (Mana-quartz).

110 AF - 160 AF: The Rift Wars

The new geopolitical reality sets in. Kingdoms realize that controlling a rift means unlimited magical power and economic dominance. Bloody, generational wars are fought over "Rift Territories."

Mercenary bands of Arcanists become the most valuable military asset in the world, rendering traditional knights largely obsolete.

185 AF: The Synthesis Heresy

A rogue faction of scholars discovers a way to forcibly graft magical crystals onto mundane weapons, armor, and even human flesh. This magitech creates terrifying new monstrosities, blurring the lines between magic and machinery.

200 AF: Summary

The world is in a tense, heavily armed standoff. The rifts are stabilized but highly guarded. The old Chantry is quietly attempting to breed the last remaining Blessed bloodlines to create an army capable of wiping out the Mages. Meanwhile, reckless Arcanists are experimenting with opening new rifts to access deeper wells of magic, risking a second cataclysm.

200-550 AF: The Age of Hubris

The reckless experimentation of the early arcanists reached its breaking point.

The Magocracies Rise: Traditional kings were overthrown entirely. The world was ruled by Archmages from massive, Aether-powered castles.

The Second Fracture (548 AF): A society of archmages attempted to artificially widen a rift to access unlimited power. The spell collapsed catastrophically. The resulting shockwave of raw Aether scoured the continent, crashing cities, shattering the great magical academies, and violently mutating the landscape.

550-1100 AF: The Dark Age of Arcane

The magical crisis plunged the world back into a brutal struggle for survival, effectively resetting the societal clock and enforcing the medieval structure.

The Scourge of the Wilds: The dense magic from the Second Rending permanently altered the world's ecosystems. A horrifying "Blight" mutations stabilized into permanent species. Dragons, gryphons, and dire-beasts became the new, natural apex predators.

Feudalism Reborn: With the great empires collapsed, humanity survived in isolated, heavily fortified settlements. Local warlords who could wield even basic battle-magic or tame the new beasts became the new feudal kings, trading protection for servitude.

1100-2300 AF: The thosand year Peace

For over a thousand years, society rebuilt into a stable, but deeply entrenched, medieval high-fantasy world.

The Guild Monopolies: Magic stopped being a wild frontier and became a highly guarded, institutionalized trade. Spells and Aether crafting are now controlled by secretive Guilds or strict Academies. You don't just "invent" magic anymore; you inherit the knowledge through years of strict apprenticeship.

The Persecution: Mages across the realms have been labeled as dangers to societies, and were outcasted or hunted down by what were now new kingdoms and Empires, greatly reducing the number of magic users across the realms.

The Current World: 2309 AF

This is the present day. The world feels ancient, built upon the bones of forgotten gods and fallen empires.

Feudal System: The world returns to a mostly feudal society. Kingdoms and Empires dominate the lands, with rare exceptions of magic societies that live in exile or sanctioned academies under strict supervision.

The State of Magic: Magic is no longer an unpredictable, chaotic force but an applied science, and strictly kept in the hands of the elite. Some swordsmen wield steel forged in magical fire, while some knights ride armored, domesticated drakes. The social stigma no longer exists on magic and its users.

The Divine: The Gods have been far less involved for 2,300 years. The Grand Chantry still exists, but mostly as a fringe group of zealots or secluded monks. However, the original Blessed Bloodlines (from Before Fracture) have become incredibly diluted but highly prized. Every few generations, a peasant might be born with an ancient, dormant Blessing (like invulnerability or true healing) making them a massive target for all, and even fewer generations, a devout servant gains a favour of the old gods.

The Forsaken (Cursed): The descendants of the originally "Cursed" humans have fully evolved into distinct, subterranean races (goblins, orcs, etc.). They have their own cultures and often wage war against the surface kingdoms, fueled by a 3,000-year-old grudge.
